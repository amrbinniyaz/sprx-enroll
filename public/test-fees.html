Bursaries

We believe that an Alleyn's education should be available to any child who would benefit from it, regardless of family income. Our bursary programme is one of the most generous in the independent school sector.

Up to 100% fee remission available

Over 15% of our pupils currently receive some form of means-tested fee assistance. Bursaries range from 5% to 100% of fees.

Who Can Apply?

Bursaries are available at all entry points (11+, 13+, and 16+). Any family whose household income is below £120,000 per year may be eligible for some level of support. Families with household income below £45,000 may qualify for a full bursary.

How Are Bursaries Assessed?

Bursary applications are assessed on a confidential basis by an independent assessor. The assessment takes into account household income, savings, investments, property equity, and any other relevant financial circumstances. Bursaries are reassessed annually.

Applying for a Bursary

To apply for a bursary, please indicate your interest on the registration form. You will then be sent a confidential financial assessment form. All bursary discussions are handled with complete discretion, and the bursary status of a pupil is never disclosed to staff or other families.

Sibling Discount

A discount of 5% on the second child's fees is available for families with more than one child at the school. A 10% discount applies to the third and subsequent children. The discount is applied to the youngest child's fees.

Forces & Diplomatic Discount

We offer a 10% fee reduction for families where a parent is serving in HM Armed Forces or the Diplomatic Service. This may be combined with the Continuity of Education Allowance (CEA).

Payment Options

We offer several convenient ways to pay school fees.

Termly Payment

Fees are invoiced before the start of each term and are due within 30 days. This is the standard payment method.

Annual Payment

Pay all three terms in advance and receive a 2% discount on total fees. Payment must be received before the start of the autumn term.

Monthly Direct Debit

Spread payments over 10 monthly instalments (September to June) via direct debit. A small administrative charge of £50 per term applies.

Fees-in-Advance Scheme

Parents may pay fees in advance for future terms at a discounted rate. Please contact the Bursar's office for current rates and details of this scheme.

Insurance

We strongly recommend that families take out school fees insurance to protect against unexpected circumstances such as redundancy, illness, or bereavement that might affect their ability to pay fees. We can provide details of approved insurers on request.

Withdrawal Notice

A full term's notice in writing is required before the withdrawal of a pupil from the school or a change of status (e.g., from full-time to part-time). In the absence of such notice, a term's fees in lieu will be charged.

Questions About Fees?

If you have any questions about fees, bursaries, or payment options, please do not hesitate to contact our Bursar's office or admissions team. All enquiries about financial assistance are handled in the strictest confidence.
