The Admissions Process

Our admissions process is designed to be straightforward and supportive. We are looking for pupils who will thrive in our stimulating and inclusive environment.

1

Register Your Interest

Complete our online registration form and pay the non-refundable registration fee of £150. We recommend registering at least 18 months before the intended entry date.

2

Attend an Open Day

Visit the school to see our campus, meet staff and current pupils, and get a feel for the Alleyn's community. Open Days run throughout the autumn term.

3

Assessment Day

Candidates attend an assessment day which includes age-appropriate tests and group activities. We are looking for potential and curiosity, not just current achievement.

4

Interview

Shortlisted candidates are invited for an interview with a senior member of staff. This is a relaxed conversation designed to get to know the child as an individual.

5

Offer

Offers are sent out in February for September entry. Successful families have two weeks to accept their place and pay the deposit.

Open Days typically run from 9:30am to 12:30pm and include a welcome talk, guided tours led by Sixth Form students, subject demonstrations, and refreshments. We strongly recommend attending before applying.

Scholarships

We offer academic, music, sport, art, and drama scholarships at 11+, 13+, and 16+ entry. Scholarships are awarded in recognition of exceptional talent and potential, and carry a fee remission of up to 10%.

Scholarship candidates are identified during the standard admissions process and invited to attend additional assessments. Music scholars, for example, are asked to perform two contrasting pieces and undertake aural and sight-reading tests.

Scholarships may be held in combination with a means-tested bursary, enabling the most talented pupils to attend Alleyn's regardless of family circumstances.

Academic Scholarships

Awarded to candidates who demonstrate exceptional intellectual ability and curiosity. Assessment is based on performance in entrance examinations and interview.

Music Scholarships

Candidates should be playing at least one instrument to a minimum of Grade 5 standard (Grade 7 for Sixth Form). Free instrumental tuition is provided.

Sport Scholarships

For pupils who show outstanding ability and commitment to sport. Candidates will attend a practical assessment and interview with the Director of Sport.

Art & Drama Scholarships

Art scholars submit a portfolio and attend a practical workshop. Drama scholars perform two contrasting monologues and participate in a group workshop.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are you looking for in a candidate?

We look for pupils who are curious, enthusiastic, and eager to learn. We value potential as much as current achievement, and we seek children who will contribute positively to our school community.

Can my child sit the assessment if they have special educational needs?

Absolutely. We make reasonable adjustments for candidates with documented learning differences. Please inform us at the time of registration so we can make appropriate arrangements.

What happens if my child is not offered a place?

If your child is not offered a place, they may be placed on our waiting list. We are also happy to provide feedback on their assessment performance. Some families choose to reapply at a later entry point.

How competitive is entry?

We typically receive around 800 applications for 120 Year 7 places, making it selective but not prohibitively so. We aim to admit children from a wide range of backgrounds and with diverse talents and interests.
